Trail Name Billy Goat Trail
Trail Length in miles 4.7
Trail DifficultyDifficult
About the Trail In Potomac, MD. Really beautiful with amazing views of the rushing Potomac River and its rocky banks. Heavily trafficked. Has three sections, A, B, and C. Section A, 1.7 miles, is the most popular and is 1.7 miles. This section has a rocky terrain and a steep climb along a cliff face. Section B is 1.4 miles, and is moderate, with minimal rock scrambling. Section C is 1.6 miles, and has no rock scrambling. Section A can be accessed from Great Falls Tavern Visitor Center. Section B and C can be accessed from Carderock, Maryland.
External Links
  • https://alltrails.com/trail/us/maryland/billy-goat-trail

  • https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billy_Goat_Trail

Trail Name Appalachian Trail: Annapolis Rock
Trail Length in miles 2.2
Trail DifficultyModerate
About the Trail One-quarter mile east of Greenbrier on Route 40. Offers an amazing view of Greenbrier Lake and Cumberland Valley from “Annapolis rock”. Continue north one mile to Black Rock, which offers a westward view of the valley. Ascent is 1700 ft. Heavily trafficked in the summer. Take blue-blazed trail from the parking lot to the white-blazed trail. Turn right to go north.
External Links
  • http://dnr2.maryland.gov/publiclands/Pages/at.aspx
